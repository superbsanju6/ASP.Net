How do I add a new Pacing Guide?

A pacing guide cannot be "added" to the system, only the shell edited.  In the beginning of a new semester Thinkgate will add a blank "shell" for every Grade/Subject/Course combination in the system.  Users can then edit these shells.  The reason you cannot add a new pacing guide is due to the issues that could be causes by a course having multiple pacing guides.
Why is there not a pacing guide available for a specific subject and course?

There should be a Pacing Guide in the system for each grade/subject/course. If one appears to be missing please contact Thinkgate Support or your district point of contact.
How do I edit a Pacing Guide?

In order to edit a pacing guide please follow the directions listed below.

 

1.Click on Curriculum
2.Click on the Find Pacing Guide Button
3.Use the criteria to search for the desired Pacing Guide
4.Click Find Pacing Guides
5.Click the Edit Button for desired Pacing Guide
6.After clicking Edit you will be brought to the Profile page of the Pacing Guide

 

For detailed instruction on how to Edit and Create a Pacing Guide please view the Pacing Guide lessons within your Reference Center.  These guides will provide step by step instruction and images on how to edit a Pacing Guide.
What are the different parts of a Pacing Guide?

Listed below are descriptions of each of the different sections within a Pacing Guide:

 

1.Standards – State Standards can be added to the Pacing Guide to ensure that all of the standards for that grade, subject and course are a part of the guide.

 

2.Instructional Schedule – This schedule outlines the standards that will be taught in each term and gives the user the option to change the teaching order.

 

3.Pacing Guide – Overview of the completed Pacing Guide.

 

4.Assessment Guide – Overview of the completed Assessment Blueprint (will not display if no Blueprint had been built).

 

For detailed instruction on how to Edit and Create a Pacing Guide please view the Pacing Guide lessons within your Reference Center.  These guides will provide step by step instruction and images on how to edit a Pacing Guide.
What does proofing a pacing guide do?

Proofing a Pacing Guide is a way of declaring that the guide is complete and ready for use.  Once a Pacing Guide has been proofed Teachers will be able to view the guide.
Can I make changes after a pacing guide is proofed?

Yes. Pacing Guides can be edited even after being proofed.

What is the difference between a pacing guide and a blueprint?

A Pacing Guide is a schedule of what is going to be taught. A Blueprint is a schedule of what the students are going to be tested on and when the assessment will occur.
Does my pacing guide transfer to my blueprint?

Pacing Guides are linked to blueprints, however Blueprints do not link back to the Pacing Guide.  If a standard is added to a Pacing Guide it will automatically be added to the blueprint.  However, if a standard is added to a blueprint it will not be added to the Pacing Guide.  Also, descriptions added to either module will be visible on both of them.
What do the blue boxes mean when I am looking at standards?

The blue boxes reflect the blueprint test schedule and indicate what term each standard will be tested in.  The separation of the Pacing Guide and Blueprint allows standards to be taught in one term and tested in another.
