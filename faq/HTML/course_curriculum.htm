Why can't classes see the district assessment I just added and proofed?  (example: User adds a Reading assessment but the Language Arts classes cannot see it to take it)

District Assessments are tied to curriculums.  Only courses with the assessment curriculum tied to them will have test events available.  For example, if a Reading class needs to take an assessment that was created under the Language Arts curriculum, this curriculum will have to be tied to the Language Arts, Reading Course.  If test events are not available in all courses needed please contact your district administrator or Thinkgate Support (if permitted).
The drop downs for Subject/Course/Curriculum in my system have a lot of options we do not use, how can I get these cleaned up?

If you notice choices in your drop downs that should not be there or do not apply to your district please contact one of the primary points of contact in your district or at your school.  They can then forward the request on to Thinkgate Support.

 

Any menu options that have prior year data tied to them cannot be removed.  This is due to accessibility through prior year reporting.
I have a class that needs to take multiple tests from different subject areas, is this possible?

Yes, the curriculums for the tests will need to be tied to the course where multiple tests from multiple subjects will be taken.  Once the curriculums are tied to the course, test events will be available in all classes with that curriculum tied.  In order to have curriculums tied to courses please contact your district administrator or Thinkgate Support (if permitted).
